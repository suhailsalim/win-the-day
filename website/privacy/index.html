Privacy Policy

Effective 21 July 2026 · Applies to the Win the Day iOS app, its Apple Watch app, its widgets, and this website.

Short version: Win the Day has no accounts, no analytics, no tracking and no servers operated by us. Your day lives on your phone. The only things that leave your device are the text you hand to the AI provider you chose, your coordinates going to a free weather service, and food searches going to a free food database.

Who we are

Win the Day is an independent app built by one developer. There is no company account system, no user database, and no analytics or advertising SDK in the app. We do not collect, receive, store, sell or share your personal data, because there is nowhere for it to arrive.

What stays on your device

Everything you log is stored locally on your iPhone:

  • Daily entries — meals, habits, hydration, workouts, study sessions, prayers, notes.
  • Plans, routines, occasions and coach chat threads.
  • Settings, targets, ring configuration and module preferences.
  • Progress photos and imported lab or body-composition documents, kept in the app's own Documents folder.
  • AI provider API keys, kept in the iOS Keychain.

Widgets and the Apple Watch app read a small shared snapshot of this data through an Apple App Group. That snapshot never leaves your devices. There is no cloud sync, so your device backup (iCloud device backup or Finder) is the app's backup.

Apple Health

With your permission the app reads Apple Health data — steps, active energy, weight and body composition, heart rate and HRV, sleep and workouts — and writes back what you log, such as calories, protein, body measurements and workouts. HealthKit access is governed entirely by iOS permissions, which you can review or revoke in the Health app at any time. The app never transmits your Apple Health data anywhere. The only exception is health text you deliberately submit to an AI feature, covered below.

What leaves your device

1. AI features — to the provider you choose

AI features only run when you invoke them, and only after you have selected a provider and supplied its API key. When you use one, the relevant text is sent directly from your device to that provider's API. That includes the meal text you are estimating, the questions and context in a coach conversation, health notes or lab and body-composition reports you submit for parsing, and photos you attach to an AI request.

The request goes from your phone to the provider — it does not pass through any server of ours. Your data is then handled under that provider's privacy policy and terms, not ours. The selectable providers are:

  • Apple Intelligence — on-device or Apple's Private Cloud Compute; nothing leaves Apple's secure path.
  • Ollama (local) — a model running on your own machine or network; nothing goes to a third party.
  • Anthropic, OpenAI, Google Gemini, OpenRouter, DeepSeek, Ollama Cloud — third-party cloud APIs.

Please read the privacy terms of whichever provider you pick. If a piece of information is too sensitive to share with a third party, use Apple Intelligence or a local Ollama model, or simply don't run an AI feature on it — every score, prayer time, plan and log in the app is deterministic and works with no AI at all.

2. Location — prayer times, Qibla and weather

If you grant location access, your coordinates are used on your device to compute prayer times and the Qibla direction — that calculation is entirely local and astronomical, with no lookup service involved. Your coordinates are sent off-device in one case only: to Open-Meteo, a free weather API, to fetch your local forecast. No account, key or identifier is attached to that request.

3. Food lookups

When you scan a barcode or search the food database, the barcode or the search term is sent to Open Food Facts, a free public food database, to fetch nutrition information. Nothing else about you is included.

Children

The app is not directed at children under 13 and collects no data from anyone, including children.

Your control

  • Revoke Health, location, calendar, reminders, notification or camera access at any time in iOS Settings.
  • Remove an API key in Settings → Intelligence to stop all cloud AI requests.
  • Deleting the app deletes its local data, including photos and its Keychain entries.

Because we hold no copy of your data, requests to access, correct, export or delete data held by us have nothing to act on — your data is already only in your hands. For data you sent to an AI provider, contact that provider directly.

This website

This site is static and hosted on GitHub Pages. It sets no cookies, loads no third-party scripts or fonts, and runs no analytics. GitHub may log ordinary web-server request data as its infrastructure provider.

Changes

If the app's data handling changes, this page changes with it and the effective date above is updated. Material changes will also be noted in the app's release notes.
